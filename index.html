Why Serbia?

Serbia, country of rivers, hills and champions

Serbia is a country blessed with an abundance of rivers and lakes, which provide ideal opportunities for holiday travel and recreation and are also perfect for cooling down during the hot summer months. Serbia boasts five national parks: Fruška Gora, Đerdap Gorge/Iron Gate, Tara, Kopaonik and Šar Mountain. In the national parks, you can set out on a quest to find some of the most endangered species of flora and fauna, enjoy breath-taking views or collect medicinal wild herbs and mushrooms.

One of the greatest tennis players of all time, Novak Djokovic, is a Serbian professional tennis player. He is currently ranked the world No. 1 player in men's singles. Denver Nuggets star center Nikola Jokic was voted the best men's Serbian basketball player in 2022. As many others: Vlade Divac, Jasna Sekaric, Milica Mandic, Ivana Vuleta, Nemanja Vidic, Filip Filipovic, Milorad Cavic..

Memories

Beautiful memories from Serbia

Belgrade

Built at the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers, Belgrade is rightly known as “the Gate of the Balkans” and “the Doors to Central Europe”.

Culture

National Museum, the Military Museum, the Museum of Contemporary Art and the Nikola Tesla Museum.

Events

Belgrade is host to traditional marathon and half-marathon, water sports, canoeing or kayaking, major international European and world basketball, handball, water polo, volleyball... Film, music and art festivals to the many exciting sports competitions, Belgrade is brimming with events!

Novi Sad

Novi Sad will softly and effortlessly win you over with its laid-back vibe, here nothing is rushed and everything happens at its own, leisurely pace.

Relax

Whether you wish to unwind on the banks of the Danube, enjoy a stroll along the city’s streets or you prefer nature and the views from the nearby Fruška Gora mountain, relaxation is guaranteed.

Ethno-cuisine

In numerous ethno-restaurants and traditional farm houses you can sample the diverse cuisine of this region. Then will you be able to truly grasp why it’s said that time passes more slowly here.

Learn more

Zlatar

Named after gold (zlato in Serbian), Zlatar fully live up to its name. It is lush with fragrant meadows and clean lakes surrounded by Serbian spruce and birch trees.

Climate

Owing to a unique blend of Mediterranean and alpine climates, the mountain boasts long annual sunshine and crisp mountain air.

Adventures

Not far away lies the canyon of the river Uvac, the habitat of a bird species unique to this part of Europe – the griffon vulture. Because of its interesting landscapes, crystal-clear water rich in fish it attracts fishermen, adventurers, nature lovers, photo-tourists...
